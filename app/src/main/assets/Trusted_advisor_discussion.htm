1.         Build Rapport

 

- Client starts the conversation with high tension i.e. Apprehensive

 

i.           Conscious rapport

“People like people who like them”

-  Common interest chit chat

- Sincere compliment/positive observation

 

ii.         Sub-Conscious rapport

“People like people who are like them”

- Work out what type of person they are:

- Dominant: High energy, Task focussed

- Influencer: High energy, People focussed

- Steady relator: Low energy, People focussed

- Cautious thinker: Low energy, Task focussed

- Use words, vocal delivery and body language to talk to that person similar to their personality type. When discussing a message pertaining to feelings and attitudes, the below communication pie shows the importance of each communication.

 

 

iii.        Agenda

- Clearly state the agenda for the meeting and get them to confirm that the agenda is ok.

- The client ends the Rapport segment with low tension i.e. Trust

 

 


2.         Questions

 

S: Situational questions

- Basic questions to determine the clients needs

- Example questions can be found here: https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/0B9sRiZcS2lwmLUhILU1tVFZUQVE

 

T: Toward questions

- Motivational tension building questioning aiming at the pleasure points of the project.

- Consists of THREE questions

 

1. Crack open question:

“What (are/would be) the key (advantages/goals/KPIs/Breakthroughs) that you are looking to (see/achieve) from this project.” Answer = “XYZ”

 

2. Drill down question 1 - Short term benefit:

“So if you can (achieve/create/establish) XYZ, what opportunity would that (mean/further give/lead to/do) for (you/the business/the team/the organisation)?” Answer = “ABC”

 

3. Drill down question 2 - Long term benefit:

“And what will ABC create long term?”

 

A: Away questions

- Motivational tensions building questioning aiming at the pain points of the project.

- Consists of THREE questions

 

1. Crack open question:

“What (are/would be) the key (issues/problems/limitations) that this project is trying to resolve?” Answer = “XYZ”

 

2. Drill down question 1 - Short term benefit:

“So if you are unable to resolve XYZ, what (adversity/threat) would that (mean/lead to) for (you/the business/the team/the organisation)?” Answer = “ABC”

 

3. Drill down question 2 - Long term benefit:

“And what would ABC do in the long term if it is not resolved?”

 

C: Criteria questions

- Drill down to the most important thing

- Consists of THREE questions

 

1.                1. “So overall, what is the MOST important thing to you about this project?” Answer = “XYZ”

2.                 

3.                2. “What is the MOST important thing to you about XYZ?” Answer = “ABC”

4.                 

5.                3. “So if you could have ABC, what will that ultimately do for YOU as a project manager/hr manager/ceo/etc…?”

 

- Note: If XYZ is “Price” then the question will need to be reasked. “I understand that price is important to you. Besides price, what is the MOST important thing to you about this project?”

 

 


3.         Solutions

 

- When offering solutions, present them in a way that follows the order below:

 

1. How our solution achieves ABC from the criteria questions

2. How our solution achieves ABC from the away questions

3. How our solution achieves ABC from the toward questions

4. How our solution fulfils the requirements of the situational questions

5. Only show the price for the solution at the very end

 

 


4.         Gain Commitment

 

- You can use Pace Pace Lead Ask (PPLA) to get a commitment for the go ahead/next meeting as below:

 

PACE = “GIVEN THAT……………………….AND”

PACE = “SEEING AS THOUGH ………………..”

LEAD = “IT MAKES SENSE THAT………………”

ASK = “………………………..?”

 

- Pace is a statement that the customer has said so it must be true

- Lead is a question or statement that you want the customer to think about

- Ask is asking a direct question about what you want
